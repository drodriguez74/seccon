## 1. Industry Overview & Market Size

The private military and security contracting (PMC/PSC) industry is one of the fastest-growing defense sectors globally.

| Metric | Data |
|---|---|
| Market size (2025) | **$254–295 billion** (varies by research firm) |
| Projected market size (2034) | **$451 billion** |
| CAGR | **5.68–7.54%** through 2034 |
| Government share of market | **61.4%** of all PMC spend |
| Commercial share | 29.7% |
| NGO / other | 8.9% |

**Growth drivers:** Increased defense outsourcing, critical infrastructure protection, rising global instability, US government reliance on contractors for expeditionary operations, and surge in demand for specialized training and advisory services.

**SecCon positioning:** Entering a $250B+ market with a government-first focus (the dominant segment) at an inflection point where smaller, agile SDVOSBs have structural contracting advantages.

## 2. SDVOSB & MBE Contracting Advantages

This is SecCon's single biggest competitive weapon. Understanding it in full shapes how the website should be positioned.

### Federal Spending Goals
- The federal government is **legally required** to award **5% of all federal contracting dollars** to SDVOSBs annually (raised from 3% by the FY2024 NDAA)
- In FY2025, agencies awarded **$28.6 billion** to SDVOSB firms across ~52,000 contract actions

### Top Awarding Agencies (FY2025)
| Agency | Awards | Share |
|---|---|---|
| **Department of Defense** | **$12.8B** | **45%** |
| Department of Veterans Affairs | $5.2B | 18% |
| Dept. of Homeland Security | $2.1B | 7% |
| General Services Administration | $1.8B | 6% |
| Health & Human Services | $1.4B | 5% |
| Department of Energy | $1.1B | 4% |

**DoD is the #1 target** — 45% of all SDVOSB contract dollars flow through DoD. SecCon is perfectly positioned.

### SDVOSB Set-Aside Mechanics
- Contracting officers can **set aside competitions exclusively for SDVOSBs**
- **Sole-source awards up to $5M** without competition (vs. $4.5M for 8(a) firms) — recently expanded by FAR revision (Sept 2025) to allow even where incumbent is an 8(a)
- ~35% of all SDVOSB awards ($10B) are **sole-sourced** — meaning no competition at all
- **Strategic Q4 window (July–September):** When agencies are behind their 5% goal, contracting officers are motivated to sole-source to SDVOSB firms to close the gap

### Security Guards Category
- "Security Guards" is a top SDVOSB contract category: **$1.6 billion** in SDVOSB awards in FY2025
- Cleared SDVOSB firms have a **significant competitive moat** — the security clearance requirement dramatically narrows the field

### MBE Advantages
- MBE certification opens access to state and local contracting programs in addition to federal
- Large prime contractors (Constellis, Booz Allen, SAIC) have **MBE subcontracting goals** — SecCon can pursue lucrative teaming arrangements and subcontracts
- Adds a diversity dimension to federal diversity, equity, and inclusion contracting requirements

**SecCon's Core Gap vs. Constellis:** UAS/cUAS, facilities/base ops, intelligence analysis, and disaster recovery. These are NOT gaps to fill immediately — they are areas to acknowledge as "future capabilities" or teaming opportunities.

## 4. In-Demand Services (2025–2026)

Based on DoD contracting trends and competitor service maps, these are the highest-demand categories SecCon should prioritize:

8. **Foreign Security Force Advising (SFA/SSR)** — Security Sector Reform is a major DoD priority
9. **UAS / Counter-UAS Awareness Training** — rapidly growing requirement; drone threats in hostile zones
10. **Cyber-Physical Security** — physical security officers need digital threat awareness
11. **Crisis Response Planning** — contingency operations consulting

## 5. Required Credibility Signals for Government Vetting

When a DoD contracting officer or program manager visits SecCon's website, they will look for these signals. **These should be on the website.**

### Tier 1 — Non-Negotiable
| Signal | What to Show |
|---|---|
| **SAM.gov Registration** | "Active SAM.gov registration" + link to profile or CAGE code |
| **CAGE Code** | Display the 5-character CAGE code visibly |
| **UEI Number** | Unique Entity Identifier (replaced DUNS) |
| **SDVOSB Certification** | SBA-certified badge, clearly displayed in header/footer |
| **MBE Certification** | MBE badge with certifying body |
| **NAICS Codes** | List primary NAICS codes (236220, 541611, 561612, 922120, etc.) |

### Tier 2 — High Impact
| Signal | What to Show |
|---|---|
| **Contract Vehicles** | OASIS+ (SDVOSB pool), GSA Schedule if obtained |
| **Security Clearance Statement** | "Personnel hold active federal security clearances" |
| **ITAR / FAR Compliance** | Explicit compliance statement |
| **CMMC** | Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (for CUI handling) |
| **Past Performance** | Even anonymized: "Trained 200+ personnel for East African embassy security" |
| **Insurance Statement** | General liability, workers comp, performance bonds |

### Tier 3 — Differentiators
| Signal | What to Show |
|---|---|
| **Capabilities Statement PDF** | Downloadable 1-page capabilities statement |
| **Leadership Bios** | Military/LE backgrounds, years of experience, certifications |
| **Training Certifications** | TCCC, ASIS CPP, NRA Law Enforcement, etc. |
| **Teaming Opportunities** | Statement welcoming subcontracting/teaming inquiries |
